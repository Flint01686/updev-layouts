About

Trivers is a global marketplace in the field of full-cycle services. The platform allows all participants of the process (Customers, Contractors, and Workers) to interact in one space.

It allows customers to easily find a qualified contractor with a good rating and reviews in any city. Receive information about the progress of work with the ability to accept or send for revision.

It also helps contractors to receive new requests from customers, without spending money on advertising and creating expensive websites, and also manage the application execution process.

Workers get the opportunity to accept requests from different contractors at the same time. Customers may evaluate and comment on the work performed, thereby forming a personal rating.

What was at the Start?

Maxim and Alexander came to us on a recommendation after an unsuccessful experience working with another IT company that developed MVP for them.

They turned to us with the following problem: the developed applications turned out to be incomplete, with a large number of bugs, complex, and not demonstrative for end users. In addition, the company has delayed the deadlines for the delivery of software twice.

At that time, they already had two mobile applications that allow digitizing the application management process on the part of the customer and on the part of the contractor, and the contractor's web application for managing applications was also already developed.

Cooperation

Duration: 6 months by T&M

From the beginning, we have built a loyal relationship with each other. We demonstrated the entire "internal kitchen", provided detailed reports on the performance, were in touch daily, and promptly responded to requests.

During the cooperation, we developed a new web application for creating requests by customers and were engaged in the completion of mobile applications.

During the development, we focused on the simplicity and intuitiveness of using the service. Since the product was initially quite complex, most of the work was on the formation of the simple basic concept. Within one month, the concept was revised several times and the product requirements were radically rewritten 4 times. The most important thing was to reduce the number of actions performed by the end-user to create a request and display complex elements in a simple form.

Special attention was paid to UX design: the stages formation of creating a request, the arrangement of elements, and the creation of intuitive elements.

Next, it is a matter of implementation under control of the project manager and the product manager. The experienced HTML developer, taking into account all possible states of elements, implemented interfaces close to pixel-perfect. Having divided the tasks, the development team implemented the product in close cooperation with the tester, configured integration with Yandex Maps, restructured the API (there was a problem of data heterogeneity, there was no single format for names and types of transmitted data) and added versioning, configured automatic deployment to the server directly from the repository.

At the same time, flexibility was preserved throughout the entire process – there was no rigid binding to the specification, changes were made on the fly and this did not cause much discomfort in the work, since much attention was initially paid to the development of the concept.
