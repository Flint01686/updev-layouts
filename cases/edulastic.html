About

Edulastic (by Snapwiz) is an educational student assessment platform trusted by teachers, school leaders, and education coaches in all 50 states of the USA. The easy-to-use platform provides teachers with a cost-effective and customizable solution for assigning digital quizzes, tests, grades, or worksheets to students.

Edulastic provides teachers with instant class data that shows who is on the right track and who needs help so that they can take action and see growth.

Challenge

  • We have added a working graphic area to draw 20 different graphic elements from the lightest (point, line, polygon, ellipse) to the most complex (polynomial, hyperbola, parabola).
  • Added the ability to move the graphic elements with the mouse and change them by points directly on the drawing area
  • We optimized the calculations to draw graphic elements – increased the algorithms speed by 3 times (from 3-4 seconds to ±1 second)
  • Implemented the drawing of several graphic elements on the same area and the interaction between the areas (understanding where the lines intersect)

What was at the Start?

Madhu (CEO) and his partner Aditya (Co-founder) founded Snapwiz back in June 2009 and raised $1.4 million in one round for the development of their products in April 2011. At the end of 2017, they had their headquarters in the United States and a staff of programmers in India, their programmers were actively working on Edulastic, but their competencies were not enough to solve complex problems, in particular related to geometry. It was to solve non-standard problems that require good knowledge in algebra and geometry that they turned to us.

Cooperation

Duration: 1.5 years by T&M

They have already come to us with a huge project containing hundreds of modules. In this project, several challenges were thrown at us: from restructuring the project architecture and fixing bugs of full-time developers to developing geometric tests, drawing the most complex graphs, and constantly optimizing algorithmic calculations.

We have developed a module for teachers to create tests, where they can create questions of 6 different types in geometry (Graphing) and 7 types based on charts. We have been working on optimizing the project architecture. The project had a problem with navigation, developers had to spend a lot of time searching for modules and components, and it was also labor-intensive to implement new components.
